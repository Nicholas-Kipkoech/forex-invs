# Trading Basics

Trading is the act of buying and selling financial assets to profit from market movements.  
Whether you trade stocks, forex, or crypto, understanding the fundamentals is key to success.

---

## What You Will Learn

- How financial markets work  
- Types of orders (market, limit, stop-loss)  
- Brokers and trading platforms  
- Reading charts and candlesticks  
- Key trading terms  

---

## 1. How Markets Work

Markets are where buyers and sellers meet to exchange assets.  
Prices are determined by **supply and demand**. Key markets include:

- **Stock Markets** – buy/sell shares of companies  
- **Forex** – currencies (USD, EUR, JPY…)  
- **Crypto** – digital assets like Bitcoin or Ethereum  
- **Commodities** – oil, gold, silver  

---

## 2. Types of Orders

| Order Type | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Market     | Buy or sell immediately at the best available price |
| Limit      | Buy or sell at a specific price or better |
| Stop-Loss  | Automatically closes a trade to limit losses |
| Take Profit| Automatically closes a trade to lock in profits |

---

## 3. Brokers & Platforms

- **Brokers** are intermediaries that allow you to trade.  
- Popular platforms: **MetaTrader 4/5**, **TradingView**, **WeBull**, **Binance**.  
- Check for: low fees, fast execution, good interface, and security.

---

## 4. Reading Charts

Charts visualize price movement over time.  
- **Candlesticks** show open, high, low, and close prices.  
- **Timeframes**: 1-min, 5-min, 1-hour, daily, weekly.  
- **Trends**: Uptrend (higher highs/lows), Downtrend (lower highs/lows).  

### Example Candlestick

┌───────┐
│ ↑ │ <- High
┌─┘ └─┐
│ │ <- Open/Close
└───────────┘ <- Low



---

## 5. Key Trading Terms

- **Liquidity** – How easily an asset can be bought/sold  
- **Volatility** – How fast prices change  
- **Leverage** – Borrowed funds to increase exposure  
- **Spread** – Difference between buy/sell price  
- **Risk Management** – Strategies to protect your capital  

---

## 6. Tips for Beginners

1. Start small – don’t risk more than you can afford to lose  
2. Learn to read charts before placing real trades  
3. Keep a trading journal to track progress  
4. Understand basic technical analysis (support/resistance, moving averages)  
5. Practice with demo accounts before going live  

---

## Conclusion

Trading requires knowledge, patience, and discipline.  
Start with a solid understanding of the basics, then gradually explore strategies and markets.  
Remember: **consistency beats luck** in the long term.
